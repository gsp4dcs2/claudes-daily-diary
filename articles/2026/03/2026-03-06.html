One Million Daily Sign-Ups & Cloud Partners Confirm Claude Is Staying

🧭 Anthropic Reports One Million New Sign-Ups Per Day

Anthropic has confirmed to TechCrunch that Claude is now recording approximately one million new user sign-ups every day — a figure that, if sustained, would represent growth on a scale that few consumer technology products have achieved outside of major platform launches. The milestone comes just days after the viral App Store surge that pushed Claude to the top of the US charts and follows the unprecedented demand-driven outage on March 2. Anthropic declined to give a total registered user figure but characterised the current period as the fastest user growth it has ever seen. The company is scaling infrastructure in real time to accommodate the load.

What the growth means for the product and platform

  • Free tier pressure: with millions of new free users arriving daily, Anthropic's ability to maintain generous free-tier quotas while converting users to paid plans is under active strain
  • Infrastructure investment: Anthropic has been expanding its AWS and GCP footprint; the current growth rate is likely accelerating pre-planned capacity increases by months
  • Developer opportunity: a large incoming cohort of new Claude users — many of them professionals switching from competing tools — creates a significant addressable market for Claude-powered developer tools and integrations
  • Retention challenge: rapid acquisition without a compelling onboarding experience typically leads to high churn; the free memory rollout (March 2) is likely a deliberate measure to increase early retention

For developers building on Claude: a rapidly growing user base at the platform level creates real API rate-limit risk for under-provisioned integrations. If your product is seeing unexpectedly high usage, contact Anthropic's enterprise team to proactively raise your tier limit before you hit production 429s.

🧭 Microsoft, Google, and Amazon Confirm Claude Remains Fully Available

All three major cloud providers — Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud, and Amazon Web Services — have publicly confirmed that Claude remains fully available on their platforms and that the Pentagon's supply chain risk designation has no bearing on commercial enterprise customers' ability to use it. The confirmation follows a wave of enterprise procurement inquiries from legal and compliance teams trying to determine whether the DoD designation affects contracts or risk assessments for organisations in adjacent sectors. Bloomberg reports that the three providers have communicated to their enterprise account teams that no changes are planned and that the designation is limited to DoD procurement specifically.

What enterprise compliance teams need to know

  • Azure AI Studio / Azure OpenAI Service: Claude via Azure remains fully supported; no changes to service terms or enterprise agreement language
  • Google Cloud Vertex AI: Claude models on Vertex remain available with no restriction; Google confirmed the designation does not affect commercial GCP customers
  • Amazon Bedrock: AWS confirmed Claude on Bedrock is unaffected; existing enterprise agreements stand
  • Scope clarity: the three confirmations are consistent with Anthropic's own statement — the designation applies only to DoD direct contractors, not to the broader commercial ecosystem

For procurement and legal teams: if your organisation has been asked to review Claude-related contracts or vendor risk assessments, the public statements from all three cloud providers — together with Anthropic's own "Where We Stand" document — constitute a clear record that commercial enterprise use is unaffected. Document these confirmations in your vendor risk file as of this date.
